Which lessons were learned from early Big Data projects such as Google Flu?

- a) The assumption that no domain knowledge is needed turned out to be wrong
- b) Spurious correlations can make models vulnerable to false dependencies
- c) Dependencies in the data may evolve over time, increasing model errors
- d) Big Data models are immune to changing data dependencies
- e) Domain expertise should always be completely excluded from Big Data work

### Explanation

Early projects showed that domain knowledge does matter (a), that spurious correlations are a real risk (b), and that evolving data dependencies (e.g. new health services) increase errors (c). The claims of immunity (d) and total exclusion of expertise (e) are the misconceptions that were disproven.
