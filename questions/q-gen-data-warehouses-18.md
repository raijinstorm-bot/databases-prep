How does a data mart typically differ from a data warehouse?

- a) A data mart stores only non-numeric data
- b) A data mart never contains any fact tables
- c) A data mart usually serves the needs of a part of an organisation rather than the entire organisation
- d) A data mart is always larger than a data warehouse

### Explanation

Unlike a data warehouse, a data mart usually serves the needs of a part of the organisation rather than the whole (c). The presentation does not restrict a mart to non-numeric data or forbid fact tables, and marts are typically smaller in scope, not larger.
