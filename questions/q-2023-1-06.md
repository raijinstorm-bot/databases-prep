The result of comparing `'abc' > NULL` is:

- a) `TRUE`
- b) `FALSE`
- c) `1`
- d) `'abc'`

### Explanation

As with `=`, ordering comparisons against NULL evaluate to UNKNOWN (not true). The condition is not satisfied, so among the offered options it behaves as FALSE.
