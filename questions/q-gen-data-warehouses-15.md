In reality, an OLAP data cube frequently includes:

- a) more than three dimensions
- b) more than one measure
- c) hierarchical dimensions allowing drill-down to find data of interest
- d) exactly three dimensions and exactly one measure
- e) raw, non-aggregated transaction logs as its only content

### Explanation

Real cubes commonly have more than three dimensions (a), more than one measure (b), and hierarchical dimensions enabling drill-down (c). The "exactly three / one measure" claim (d) is just the simplified textbook picture, and cubes serve aggregated analytical data rather than raw logs (e).
